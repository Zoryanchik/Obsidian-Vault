Console logging is a central feature of coding/debugging with Javascript. console.log is very versatile and can receive multiple primitives/objects and display them in the console for further investigation. In addition to console.log, other logging functions are also available. _console.info()_, _console.warn()_, _console.error()_, _console.debug()_
### 1️⃣ `console.log()`

`console.log("g");`

- **Purpose**: General-purpose logging.
    
- Most common for printing any messages or values.
    
- Example output in console:
    

`g`

---

### 2️⃣ `console.info()`

`console.info("d");`

- **Purpose**: Information messages.
    
- Often styled differently in some browsers (e.g., with an info icon or color).
    
- Example output:
    

`d`

---

### 3️⃣ `console.warn()`

`console.warn("e");`

- **Purpose**: Warning messages — things that **might indicate a problem**.
    
- Usually shown with a yellow triangle ⚠️ in dev tools.
    
- Example output:
    

`e  (with warning icon)`

---

### 4️⃣ `console.error()`

`console.error("b");`

- **Purpose**: Error messages — critical problems in code.
    
- Usually shown in **red with an error icon**.
    
- Some browsers also show a stack trace.
    
- Example output:
    

`b  (with error icon)`

---

### 5️⃣ `console.debug()`

`console.debug("u");`

- **Purpose**: Debugging messages — sometimes filtered out in production or depending on console settings.
    
- Often hidden unless the console is in “verbose” or “debug” mode.
    
- Example output:
    

`u`
