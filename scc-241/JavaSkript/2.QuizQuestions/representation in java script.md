### **a. `1.256`** ✅

- Standard decimal number.
    
- Valid syntax.
    
- **Result:** `1.256`
    

---

### **b. `120E6`** ✅

- Scientific notation: `E` means “times 10 to the power of”.
    
- `120E6` = `120 × 10⁶`
    
- Valid in JavaScript.
    

---

### **c. `-133`** ✅

- Standard negative integer.
    
- Valid numeric literal.
    

---

### **d. `0X34g`** ❌

- Hexadecimal literals start with `0x` or `0X` and **must only contain digits 0–9 and letters a–f (or A–F)**.
    
- `g` is not a valid hexadecimal digit.
    
- **Invalid.**
    

---

### **e. `12.5e1`** ✅

- Scientific notation with decimal: `12.5e1` = `12.5 × 10¹ = 125`
    
- Valid numeric literal.
    

---

### **f. `0x123`** ✅

- Valid hexadecimal literal: `0x123` = `291` in decimal.
    

---

### **g. `12b6`** ❌

- Not a valid number in JavaScript.
    
- `b` is not part of decimal, hexadecimal, or binary notation here.
    
- **Invalid.**
    

---

### **h. `13e`** ❌

- `e` in scientific notation must be followed by an **exponent number**.
    
- `13e` is incomplete (no number after `e`).
    
- **Invalid.**
