Capacity is essentially the ability of a system, organization, or resource to **perform work** or **handle output**. It is often measured in terms of volume, throughput, or resource availability.
![[Pasted image 20250204011148.png]]
Capacity refers to the **maximum amount of work, goods, or services** that can be produced or handled within a certain time frame using the available resources. It’s about how much a system, organization, or machine can manage.

Think of it as:

- **Throughput**: How much work you can get done.
- **Volume**: How much you can hold, process, or produce.

### **Level Capacity Planning**

**Definition**:  
In level capacity planning, production levels remain **constant** regardless of demand fluctuations. The organization maintains a steady output and doesn’t adjust the workforce, equipment, or other resources based on demand changes.

![[Pasted image 20250204012740.png]]
### **Demand Chase Planning**

**Definition**:  
In demand chase planning, production is adjusted to **match demand fluctuations** as closely as possible. The organization alters its capacity by changing the workforce, production hours, or equipment use.
