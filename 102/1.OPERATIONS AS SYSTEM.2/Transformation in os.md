- **Types of Processors:**
    
    - Organizations transform different entities into outputs, categorized into three types:
        - **Materials processors:** Industries such as **manufacturing, mining, and energy** transform raw materials or physical goods.
        - **Information processors:** Professions such as **accountancy, media, and research** focus on processing and managing information.
        - **Customer processors:** Sectors like **hotels, hospitals, education, and transport** provide services that directly affect or involve customers.

**Project Operation:**
- Focused on one-off, highly specialized products with significant customization.
- Typically used for unique, large-scale endeavors like building a bridge or a spacecraft.
**Jobbing Operation:**
- Produces highly customized, small-scale items in low volumes.
- Each product may be slightly different, requiring flexibility and skilled labor.
**Batch Operation:**
- Produces a set quantity of identical products in one batch before switching to another product.
- More standardization compared to jobbing, but still allows some customization.
**Mass Operation:**
- Involves large-scale production of standardized products with minimal customization.
- Highly repetitive and efficient.
**Continuous Operation:**
- Used for non-stop, high-volume production of products like chemicals or energy.
- Suitable for homogeneous outputs.
